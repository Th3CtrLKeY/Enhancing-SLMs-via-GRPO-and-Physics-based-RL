3.15 Potential Flow and Kutta Condition 
From the P-Flow solution for ﬂow past a body we obtain 
P-Flow solution, inﬁnite velocity at trailing edge. 
Note that (a) the solution is not unique - we can always superimpose a circulatory ﬂow 
without violating the boundary conditions, and (b) the velocity at the trailing edge → 
∞. We must therefore, impose the Kutta condition, which states that the ‘ﬂow leaves 
tangentially the trailing edge, i.e., the velocity at the trailing edge is ﬁnite’. 
To satisfy the Kutta condition we need to add circulation. 
Circulatory ﬂow only.
