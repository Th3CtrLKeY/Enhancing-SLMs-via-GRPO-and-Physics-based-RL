Boundary layer thickness is related to the area where the viscosity and vorticity eﬀects are 
diﬀused. 
For a ﬂow over an inﬁnite ﬂat plate, the boundary layer thickness increases unless it is 
constrained in the y direction and/or by time (unsteady ﬂow).

If the ﬂow is constrained in y, eventually the viscous eﬀects are diﬀused along the 
entire cross section and the ﬂow becomes invariant in the streamwise direction.
