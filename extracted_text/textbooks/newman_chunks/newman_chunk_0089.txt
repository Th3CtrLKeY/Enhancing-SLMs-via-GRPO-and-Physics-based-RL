Replac­ ing the velocity by its potential may at first seem an unnecessary com­ plication, since we can envisage the velocity and measure it with suitable instruments in the laboratory, whereas the velocity potential is no more than a mathematical abstraction. However, the velocity is a vector quantity with three unknown scalar components, whereas the velocity potential is a single scalar unknown from which all three velocity components may be computed.
