A Cartesian coordinate system (x, y, z) is adopted, with y = 0 the plane of the undisturbed free surface and the y-axis positive upward. The vertical elevation of any point on the free surface may be defined by a function y = η(x, z, t). In the special case of two-dimensional fluid motion,1 parallel to the x-y plane, the dependence on z will be deleted.
