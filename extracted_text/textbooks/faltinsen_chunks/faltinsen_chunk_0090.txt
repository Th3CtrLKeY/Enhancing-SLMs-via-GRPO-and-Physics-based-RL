To evaluate the transverse viscous current force and current yaw
moment on the ship one can use the cross-flow principle as long as the
current direction is not close to the longitudinal axis of the ship. The
cross-flow principle assumes that the flow separates due to cross-flow
past the ship, that the longitudinal current components do not influence
the transverse forces on a cross-section, and that the transverse force on
a cross-section is mainly due to separated flow effects on the pressure
distribution around the ship. This means we write the transverse current
