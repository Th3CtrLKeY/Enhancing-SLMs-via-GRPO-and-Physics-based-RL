Bearman et al. (1979) presented measurements of a series of twodimensional bodies in plane oscillatory flow for KC-values between 3
and 70. They concentrated on bluff bodies with sharp-edged separation
and measured the in-line force on a flat plate, square, diamond and
circular cylinder. Beyond a KC-value of about 10 to 15 the curves for the
flat plate, circular and diamond cylinders are remarkably similar, and by
KC= 50 the Cp-values are all only a little higher than their steady flow
values, Cp”. To demonstrate this similarity, the values of Cp above
KC = 10 are shown plotted in Fig. 7.12 where they have been divided by
their steady flow value. The square section cylinder showed a different
trend.
