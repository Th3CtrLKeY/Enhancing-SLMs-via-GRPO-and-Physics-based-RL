It follows from the discussion of equation (5.3) that there are non-linear
effects due to the quadratic velocity term in Bernoulli’s equation that can
create excitation forces with higher frequencies than the dominant
frequency components in a wave spectrum. This is due to terms
oscillating with frequencies 2w,, 2w, and (w,+ 2). These may be
important for exciting the resonance oscillations in the heave, pitch and
roll of a tension leg platform. However, it can be shown that the
contribution from the quadratic velocity term in Bernoulli’s equation to
the sum-frequency heave forces for a tension leg platform is normally
small at the natural heave period in heave. This follows from the
exponential depth decay of this term, together with the fact that it is the
pontoons and the bottom of the columns that normally contribute to the
vertical force on a TLP and that the natural periods are from 2 to 4s.

If the two frequencies w, and mw, are equal, we note that the

In the case of regular waves incident on a two-dimensional body, the
reflected waves will interact with the incident waves in the way
