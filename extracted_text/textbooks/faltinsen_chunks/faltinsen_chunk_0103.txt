Overvik (1982) (see Fig. 6.32) saw galloping clearly in some of his tests
for riser bundles. It happened typically for Up > 10. Galloping motions
may also occur for ships at a single point mooring system or for a ship
which is towed (see exercise 6.3).

The galloping motions are_not self- limited as are ‘lock-in’ oscillations.
Actually at high velocities the amplitude of oscillation is proportional to
the incident velocity. This also seems to be the case in Overvik’s tests
(see Fig. 6.32). On the other hand there are limitations on how large Up
can be in Fig. 6.32 in, practical cases. We will discuss this. Let us use
Fig. 6 6.28 which shows the natural periods for the structure shown in Fig.
6.27. Galloping will not occur for this riser, but let us use the natural
periods as indicative of the natural periods for a riser bundle where
problem may occur. We can write Up=T,U,/D and assume U,=
lms~' and D=1m. From Fig. 6.28 we note that the highest natural
period 7, is less than 10s when the water depth is 100m. This means
that Up < 10. Experience shows that there will not be any large amplitude
due to. galloping motion when Up < 10. However, for 500 m water depth
the Up-value corresponding to the first eigenmode is larger than 30. This
means the possibility of galloping should be considered.
